What verification changed

Nine corrections

Before any claim reached these pages, each was checked against an independent source. Nine claims changed in that pass. All nine are here — the first-pass wording, the correction, and the source that forced it. The first row is a quote the first pass attributed to a real Target executive that turned out to exist in no source. Verification caught it and pulled the quotation marks. Not every correction was a rewrite: at least one was resolved by deletion — the "margin recovery is being bought" conclusion was withdrawn, not restated, once the adjusted-basis math reversed it.

What the first pass hadThe correctionWhat caught it
Target CIPO Prat Vemana quoted as saying Target was "expanding the use of AI agents across merchandising, inventory and digital marketing." The quotation marks came off. That sentence is in none of the cited interviews. Vemana's real, sourced quotes on Target's agentic work were used instead. A line-level scan of the CIO Dive republication returned zero matching sentences; an exact-phrase search returned nothing.
A paraphrase of CEO Michael Fiddelke's "one good quarter" line. Replaced with his exact words: "a single good quarter has never been our goal." Retail Dive, corroborated by WWD.
Target's ~30 bps FY2025 gross-margin decline read as the tariff cost the company absorbed. The 30 bps is the total margin decline. The CFO separately sized one-time tariff and inventory costs near $0.5B and said margin would have expanded without them — so the tariff drag was larger than 30 bps, not equal to it. The FY2025 10-K rate table against the Financial Community Meeting transcript (Jim Lee).
The "step function change" phrase attributed to the Jeff England appointment press release. That press release does not contain the phrase. COO Lisa Roath said it on the Q1 2026 earnings call. The press release was fetched and searched; the phrase turned up in the Q1 2026 transcript instead.
Roundel drove "nearly $2 billion" in value in 2024. "More than $2 billion" — the wording in Target's own wire copy. The March 4, 2025 PR Newswire release.
250 million Rufus shoppers and $10B+ incremental sales, treated as a 2025 full-year figure. Those were year-to-date, from Amazon's Q3 2025 call. The full-year figures are 300M+ customers and ~$12B annualized. The earlier numbers now carry their November-2025 date. Fortune (Nov 2025) against Amazon's Q4 2025 reporting.
Target's margin "recovery is being bought" — a 4.5% operating margin against a prior-year 6.2%. The prior-year 6.2% was GAAP, lifted by a one-time interchange-settlement gain. On the adjusted basis (3.7%), the 4.5% expanded about 80 bps. The "being bought" conclusion reversed and was withdrawn. Target's Q1 2026 release and the SEC 8-K exhibit.
Paul Smith's Microsoft Technology Center engagement described in the present tense. Moved to past tense; that center is now the Microsoft Innovation Hub. A live re-scrape of the Microsoft page.
Paul Smith listed at ServiceNow, "CCO 2022–present," per ServiceNow's executive-team page. That page is stale. Smith left ServiceNow in 2025 and was named Anthropic's Chief Commercial Officer in July 2025. Anthropic's own appointment announcement; the ServiceNow page still listed departed executives.

Considered and excluded, for the record: two widely reported enterprise cost claims (Uber's AI-tools budget, Microsoft's Claude Code licenses) were researched and left out because the companies involved never confirmed them. The register carries what would survive an audit; exclusions are part of that.
