The post-engagement record

One executive briefing cycle, run end to end

This site is the queryable record of a complete briefing cycle: intake, discovery, agenda, the room, and the follow-through. It was built as a working sample for a hypothetical engagement with Target, by one person and Claude, from public information only.

Why this exists. I'm applying to help build Anthropic's Executive Briefing Center. I could describe what I'd do in the role, but I learn faster by doing the work, so I researched how Anthropic runs briefings and built one full cycle myself: intake, discovery, agenda, the room, follow-through. Everything comes from public information; Target wasn't involved. Nine claims didn't survive my own verification pass, and the sources page shows every one. If you have three minutes, read the agenda page, then search the record below.

What a live cycle has that this sample can't

Honesty about the ceiling: a real cycle runs on account-team intelligence, the customer's own data and answers, and an actual room with actual reactions, none of which public record can supply. The discovery here is real research with real verification, but the commitments are a design. No deal sits behind them. Run #2 changes two things this sample only gestures at: the discovery form goes to a real customer, and the measurement system starts producing a number someone has to defend. That is the point at which this stops being a sample and starts being a program. And the customer relationship stays the account team's throughout: a briefing program runs inside their ownership of the account, it doesn't stand in for it.

The model layer, on the record

A live record would also carry the model layer itself. Anthropic publishes its model lifecycle in the open: deprecation notices on at least 60 days' warning (Sonnet 4 and Opus 4 retired June 15, 2026), and engineering postmortems when an infrastructure bug degrades output quality.26 A live program record tags the Claude models and endpoints behind each account's deployments, the way a supply chain tags lots. When a retirement date is announced, exposure reads straight off the record and the program walks into the account with a migration plan already drafted. When a postmortem lands, were we affected, and in which window becomes a minutes-long lookup, and the sponsor hears it from the program first.

Built July 2026. The subject was chosen for the quality of its public record: a Fortune-50 retailer, five months into a new-CEO turnaround, with technology named as a top-three priority and budget attached — the textbook profile of an executive-briefing customer.
