Phase 1 · Intake & qualification

A briefing is rationed, not booked

The cycle opens with a request, a qualification decision against a stated bar, and an account-team alignment call. Duration: request to go/no-go, 2 business days.

The briefing request

Requested byGlobal Account Director, Retail (the account team owns the request channel; the program holds the bar)
AccountTarget Corporation: Fortune-50 retailer, ~$105B net sales, ~440,000 team members, 2,000+ stores1
Stage in evaluationEarly architecture & partner selection. The CEO has named technology his third priority with an ongoing budget step-up "including AI"; no enterprise AI architecture has been publicly committed2
Executive attendees (role titles; see note)Chief Operating Officer · Chief Global Supply Chain & Logistics Officer · Chief Information Officer function · SVP Merchandising Operations · VP Enterprise Strategy
Requested windowWithin 45 days, ahead of holiday-season planning lock

Attendees are stated as role titles only. Where this cycle cites Target executives (Fiddelke, Roath, England), it cites their public statements only — all three are on the record in the company's 2026 disclosures.3

The gap that qualifies the briefing

A briefing is not a meeting. It qualifies when the customer holds a stated gap between an undesired result they have named and a desired result they are funding, and when the people who own that gap will be in the room.

The undesired result, in Target's own words

"Product findability and in-stock availability remain the biggest friction points for our guests" — the Chief Operating Officer, on the most recent earnings call.3 The CEO's own grade: "we still haven't arrived."2 Behind it: demand-forecast volatility the company says it is "working to use AI to improve."3

The desired result: funded, on the record

"Accelerating technology" is the CEO's stated priority #3, backed by a $1B ongoing P&L step-up that explicitly includes "technology, including AI" ("not one-time costs") on top of ~$5B capital expenditure.2 A new Chief Global Supply Chain & Logistics Officer was hired with a public mandate for "step function change."3

The gap between those two paragraphs is the briefing's reason to exist. Everything downstream (discovery, agenda, the room, the follow-through) exists to close it by one committed step.

Qualification decision

BarEvidenceMet
Multi-executive audience with decision authorityFirst-year COO and supply-chain chief, both with fresh public mandates✓

Decision: qualified. Declined requests get a smaller intervention: a working session with the account team. The bar is what makes the briefing worth an executive's day.

The alignment call

Before any content exists, a 30-minute call with the account team captures three things, each with a done-condition:

CapturedDone when
Who is attending: every seat, seniority, and functionAttendee roster confirmed in writing by the account team
What each attendee needs: their objectives, in their termsA move-from → move-to line exists per attendee (see Discovery)
The strategic priorities they execute: the company agenda each attendee carriesEach objective mapped to a stated corporate priority, with a source
Under the hood: how this phase was actually produced

In a live cycle, the request form arrives from the account team and the qualification note is drafted against their account plan. In this sample, Claude assembled the request profile and the gap statement from Target's public record (the 10-K, two earnings-call transcripts, and the March 2026 investor meeting) in a single research pass, and the program manager's job was judgment: which of five candidate gaps was load-bearing enough to carry a briefing.
