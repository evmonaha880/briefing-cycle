Phase 4 · Delivery

The room runs on demos and walls. The deck is what travels.

In the room, capture is a live duty: action items are noted between sessions, in front of the customer, and recapped at close. What follows is the briefing document itself, written to be read and forwarded; it travels without a presenter.

The briefing document

Nine pages. Every chart title states an observation. Every number footnoted; the two flagged claims carry their flags into the page.

The point, first

Target has spent 14 months rebuilding itself for speed. The next constraint is informational. It is addressable within this fiscal year.

The acceleration office, the flattened organization, and three first-year executives removed the structural friction. What remains is the friction inside the work itself: forecasts that wobble, availability that disappoints on weekend evenings, sourcing decisions re-made monthly under tariff churn, and thousands of back-office hours that go to assembling context before any deciding can start. That is the class of friction agentic AI removes, and the readiness work is already done.

Framing built entirely from Target's public record; sources on each page following.

Act I · The mirror

The inflection is real. Durability is the open question. Your CEO says so himself.

Q1 2026: comps +5.6%, traffic-led, broad-based; guidance raised to ~4%. And yet: shares fell ~4% on the beat, the guide implies ~1% comps for the remaining year, the two-year stack sits at +3.7%, and Q2 laps the year's hardest comparison. "A single good quarter has never been our goal. Our goal is consistent long-term growth."

Read plainly: momentum has been bought with merchandising newness and price moves. Durability requires structural levers, and the executive team's public language says it knows.

Q1 2026 release + call transcript; Retail Dive (verified CEO wording); market reaction per CNBC. Full citations: sources 3, 5.

Act I · The named pain

In-stocks are your stated #1 guest friction. 2022 is why forecasting is a board-level nerve.

The COO's words: "product findability and in-stock availability remain the biggest friction points for our guests." The CEO's grade: "we still haven't arrived." The memory underneath is 2022, when inventory peaked at $15.1B (+43% YoY) and one quarter later operating income fell 87%. That was the costliest forecasting miss in the company's modern history. The 2026 counter-evidence is real (turns +10%, cleaner inventory), and the company is now chasing inventory into raised guidance under record-low consumer sentiment. It is the 2022 setup, in reverse.

Q1 2026 transcript (including the consumer-sentiment discussion in the call's Q&A); SEC-filed 2022 exhibits. Sources 3, 6.

Act II · The clock

The competitive set is already deploying. The gap is deployment muscle, not model access.

Walmart runs a customer-facing agent in production in its app, reports ~900,000 associates on conversational AI tools (~3M questions weekly), embedded its agent into ChatGPT and Gemini after a first partnership underperformed, and framed its own CEO succession around AI. Amazon's shopping agent reached 300M+ customers with nearly $12B in annualized incremental sales attributed during 2025.

The counter-beat: Costco and TJX are taking share from Target with no AI narrative at all.25 AI is a lever, not a religion, which is exactly why the useful question is which architecture, which partner, which use cases first.

Walmart July 2025 announcement + FY26 reporting (at announcement, one of four agents was fully live); Walmart CEO succession per the SEC-filed announcement; Amazon Q4 2025 full-year figures; share arithmetic per GlobalData via CNBC. Sources 12–13, 25.

Act II · The task evidence

Measured across millions of real AI conversations: the fastest-growing lane is exactly the work your back office does.

From Anthropic's Economic Index, task-level findings across real usage: office and administrative support tasks rose three percentage points in a single quarter to 13% of first-party API transcripts ("businesses are increasingly using Claude to automate routine back-office workflows"); 93% of conversations produce a concrete artifact: a document, a report, a plan. And the whitespace cuts the other way: frontline and physical work remain the least AI-exposed occupations, meaning the first wave lands in the back office, merchandising ops, and planning, well before it reaches the store floor.

One more finding, offered with its own caveat attached: the Index's baseline implies ~1.8 points of annual labor-productivity growth over the next decade, roughly halved to ~1.0 once task reliability is priced in.

Anthropic Economic Index, Jan + Jun 2026 reports: occupation/task-level patterns; the industry application is this briefing's own synthesis. Source 14.

Act III · The staircase

A staircase for retail operations: one rung at a time, named proof at each rung.

Rung 1 · task augmentation, back office: documents, vendor correspondence, compliance drafting. Proof class: the task-level usage data on the previous page — the back-office lane is measurably where enterprise AI work is growing fastest.
Rung 2 · workflow agents, merch & vendor ops: supplier QA, certification checks, launch documentation. Proof: Lotte Homeshopping's 24/7 partner-support agent, corroborated by two platforms.
Rung 3 · analytics orchestration: L'Oréal runs Claude as the orchestrator of 15+ specialized agents; 44,000 monthly users on conversational analytics, 99% accuracy ⚠️ vendor-published figures.
Rung 4 · supply-chain orchestration: demand sensing, allocation, import planning; the rung this briefing's pilots point at, reached by climbing the rungs below it.

The same climb is underway at the customer edge — Shopify's merchant assistant runs on the same model family ⚠️ vendor-published — but this briefing's staircase is the operational one, because that is where Target's named pains live.

Anthropic retail guide + customer pages; Lotte corroborated by Sendbird and AWS. Source 15.

Act III · Trust, demonstrated

You have lived what third-party access failure costs. Your AI partner should be chosen like it matters.

Target's 2013 breach entered through a vendor's stolen credentials and ended in a settlement with 47 states and D.C., and executive departures. The company rebuilt its security posture from that wound. That history is why a briefing about customer-adjacent AI leads with the governance facts, the material usually saved for legal review: enterprise data is not used for model training by default; SOC 2 and ISO-class certifications; and a partner whose stated posture is remaining "unconflicted": no advertising business pulling the model toward anything but the work.

Breach history: public settlements record. Product terms: Claude Enterprise page. Positioning: CCO public statements, Feb 2026. Sources 16–17.

Act IV · The commitments

Three pilots, sequenced: each scored on numbers Target already reports.

Pilot 1 · Demand-forecast & in-stock assist (supply chain). Sponsor: the supply-chain organization, whose new chief carries a public "step function change" mandate. Success criteria drawn from Target's own scoreboard: in-stock availability in named categories and dayparts, inventory-turn trendline.
Pilot 2 · Import & tariff planning agent (sourcing). The company sources roughly half its merchandise internationally and is importer of record for most of its owned brands, mid-glide from 60% to under 25% own-brand China sourcing, with ~$0.5B of one-time tariff costs absorbed last year: a planning workload built for an agent with a human on the decisions.
Pilot 3 · Back-office operations automation. The Index-backed fastest lane; unglamorous by design: "the fastest ROI often comes from operational workflows," in the retail guide's own words.15

Each pilot carries a charter: scope contained enough to deliver quickly, an enthusiastic named sponsor, clear data availability, and graduation criteria written on day one, so no pilot can succeed quietly and go nowhere. Two disciplines govern the sequence. Plan slow, deliver fast: the pilot is the cheap, reversible simulation; the expensive window is production, so pilots exist to make the production run short. And each pilot is chosen as a repeatable module in a self-referencing niche: one team whose results the adjacent teams can't dismiss, because that adjacency is the mechanism by which value spreads.24

Pilot filter and graduation-criteria language: Anthropic's published enterprise playbook. Scoreboard: Target FY2026 guidance + stated operational metrics. Sources 2, 18.

After the room

The briefing ends. The cycle doesn't.

Within days: the action plan with owners and dates, in customer-benefit language. Within a month: the first sponsor check-in and a signed pilot charter. Within ninety days: an executive review against the graduation criteria, and a record of the whole cycle that anyone on either side can query without asking. That machinery is Phase 5, and it is the part most programs skip.
